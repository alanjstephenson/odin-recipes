Pizza Recipe

Check out this super simple pizza recipe!

This pizza recipe is super simple, takes no time at all and is made entirely from scratch. If you're used to store bought pizza, you're going to have your mind blown!

This recipe doesn't take a lot of work time but requires an early start to let the dough rise. I make this for me and my doughter all of the time and is perfectl portioned for one child sized pizza and one hungry adult!

Recipe

Equipment

  • 1 Mixing bowl
  • Medium bowl
  • Wooden spoon
  • Scraper/Spatular
  • Cheese grater
  • Hand blender
  • Scales

For the Base

  • 220g of plain flour
  • 140g of warm water (weight your water for accuracy!)
  • 6g of sugar
  • 4g of salt
  • 7g (1 packet) of instant yeast
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil

For the toppings

  • 1 tin of chopped tomatoes
  • Dried oregano
  • Garlic powder
  • Grated cheddar Cheese

Instructions

Follow these instructions to the letter for a perfect pizza every time!

Pizza Base

  1. Dissolve the sugar in the warm water in the medium bowl
  2. Mix the yeast into the water mixture and leave in a warm place for 10 minutes until it activates

    3. You'll know it's ready and active when it looks all frothy on top!

  3. Combine your flour and sugar in the mixing bowl and make a well in the middle
  4. Once your yeast is activated, pour the olive oil into the flour, followed by the water mixture
  5. Mix with a wooden spoon until roughy combined and pour onto clean work surface. Scrape the sides of bowl with spatula to ensure all of the mixture is on the bench
  6. Bring the mixture together eith your hands until it forms a dough
  7. Knead the dough for 10 minutes to build strength. This is important for when you're stretching it into a base later
  8. Grease a clean mixing bowl with 1tsp of olive oil and put your dough inside. Cover it with a tea towel and leave in a warm place for 1 hour or until roughly doubled in size
  9. Once the dough has doubled in size, turn it out onto a clean surface
  10. Split the dough into roughly 2/3 and 1/3 to give you an adult portion and child portion
  11. Line two trays with baking paper and lightly flour them. Take your portioned dough and squash them down into a patty shape before turning them over, ensuring there is flour underneath once more.
  12. Push the dough down, stretching it withh your hands into the shape you desire until your pizza base is complete!

Pizza toppings

  1. Now that your pizza base is ready, make sure your oven is set to 200c, fan oven
  2. Empty a tin of tomatoes into a container, I use a protein shaker bottle
  3. Season to your taste with dried oregano and garlic powder then blend, be generous!
  4. Take 3/4 spoonfuls and put in the centre of your adult pizza, less for the childs portion. Spead it around the base of the pizza using the back of the spoon
  5. Cover the pizza base with grated cheese and place your pepperoni
  6. Cook your pizzas for between 12-15 minutes depending on taste then slice and place up!
